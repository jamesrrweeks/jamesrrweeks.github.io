The Relationship Principle

Everyone has objects that are sentimental to them, things that they can’t be parted with. These products harbour more emotional attachment than any other because they are associated with stories, moments and understanding. These products are extremely personal and consequently can’t intentionally be designed. The moment something becomes curated in the attempt of seeming personal it ruins the personality.

The ultimate personal product is a product that ages with the user. A shoe for example, moulds to the shape of your foot over a long period of time, becoming personal. A personal product is one that ages with its user. Put simply, the longer an object stays in someone's life the greater the personal connection they develop with it.

Case Study

Altering the Onzo business model to extend the duration of an experience can open the possibility of building greater emotional attachments with the bikes. A subscription service expands on the idea of what a bike share can be whilst remaining within the realms of a product-service system. With a subscription, users can start and finish the service whenever they like.

Repair and maintenance will be included within the service. When products meet the end of their working lives they go back to the manufacturer to be repurposed. Considering the Relationship Principle means extending the time the user spends with the product to encourage emotional attachment, kaitiakitanga and care.
